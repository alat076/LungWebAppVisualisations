Lung Diseases

In people with an obstructive lung disease, their airways are obstructed, making it harder to exhale all of the air in their lungs.

In people with a restrictive lung disease, their lungs are restricted from filling entirely with air. They cannot expand to their optimal volume.

Common Symptoms of Lung Diseases

  • Dyspnoea (breathlessness)
  • Wheezing/coughing
  • Sputum (phlegm) hypersecretion
  • Fatigue (tiredness)
  • Anxiety/depression

Aggravations

Most lung diseases are progressive, meaning they worsen overtime. Contributing factors may include:

  • Smoking
  • Occupational dust exposure
  • Longstanding asthma
